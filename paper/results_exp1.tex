% ---- prose ----

Table~\ref{tab:exp1} compares both flow planners, raw and under every
calibrator, against the classical baselines. Three observations. First, the
safety layer is what makes the generative planner competitive: raw flow
matches the human-blind A$^*$ profile (54\% SR, 43\% collision episodes),
while every calibrated variant exceeds the RVO2 expert's success rate, with
state-conditioned ACI surpassing it by five points (64\% vs.\ 59\%) --- though
the expert retains the lowest collision rate (6\%). Second, conditioning the
planner on the same Social-LSTM forecasts the disks calibrate acts as a
safety multiplier: at essentially unchanged success (60--61\%), it roughly
halves the collision rate under every calibrator (9--14\% vs.\ 17--21\%),
yielding a Pareto pair --- state+ACI for task performance (64\%/17\%),
S-LSTM+ACI for safety (60\%/9\%). Third, the self-tuning variants are free:
DtACI matches hand-tuned ACI and MaxDt$^{+}$ matches Max$^{+}$ within noise
in both planners, so the knob-free calibrators can be used as defaults
without sacrificing performance.
